I am an Afghan girl, trying to find hope and purpose in a place where opportunities seem to vanish each day. I studied until the 11th grade, deeply committed to my education, but my dreams were cut short. When the Taliban took control of Afghanistan, they took away our right to learn, and I was no longer able to attend school. It has been heartbreaking to see my future slipping through my fingers.

Even with these difficult circumstances, I haven’t stopped dreaming. I am trying every day to find a way to continue my studies, searching for scholarships in other countries, especially in the United States or Canada, where I could be free to learn and grow. But without a 12th-grade education, so many doors remain closed. No college, university, or even high school has been able to offer me a scholarship because of my incomplete schooling. It’s a painful reminder of how much has been taken from me, just because of where I was born.

My greatest wish is to receive a scholarship, to find a place where I can learn again, and to build a future I can be proud of. I have always dreamed of studying in Canada or the United States, where education is valued and students are given the freedom to pursue their ambitions. Though the path is difficult, I hold onto my hope. My heart is filled with determination, and I believe that one day, despite all these hardships, I will make my dream a reality.

Bahara Ibrahimi
